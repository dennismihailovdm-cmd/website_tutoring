How often should my child have lessons?

+
  • Once a week is the sweet spot for most students
  • Twice a week is recommended closer to exam season or if your child needs a bigger boost
  • We'll advise you on the right frequency after the first session — every child is different

How do I get started?

+
  • Simply get in touch via the contact page and give us a call
  • We'll have a quick chat about your child's needs, targets, and current level
  • From there we'll start teaching!

What if my child is struggling with confidence, not just the content?

+
  • This is more common than you'd think — and it's just as important to us as the academic side
  • We build confidence gradually through small wins, positive reinforcement, and a no-pressure environment
  • Many of our students come in nervous and leave believing in themselves — that shift matters more than any grade

How far in advance should we start 11+ prep?

+
  • Ideally Year 4 — this gives plenty of time to cover all four subjects thoroughly
  • Starting in Year 5 is absolutely fine too, especially for a focused boost
  • Even a few months of targeted prep can make a real difference — it's never too late to start

What age groups do you teach?

+
  • 11+ preparation — typically Years 4 and 5 (ages 8–10)
  • GCSE — Years 7 to 11 (ages 11–16)
  • A-Levels — Years 12 and 13
  • Not sure if we cover your child's year group? Just ask — we're happy to help

How do online lessons work?

+
  • Lessons are held on Zoom with cameras on for both tutor and student
  • We use screen sharing, a live whiteboard, and pen annotations
  • Your child can write directly on screen — making it just as hands-on as sitting in the room
  • All you need is a device, a stable internet connection, and somewhere quiet to sit

Practice Test

Test your knowledge — select the best answer for each question.

Every question in this test is taken directly from real 11+ past papers — this is exactly the style, difficulty, and format your child will face on exam day. Work through all 14 questions, then submit to get an honest picture of where your child stands right now.

11+ Maths Diagnostics Test

Q1) Anna has £25. She buys a novel priced £8.99, a box of coloured pencils priced £4.25, and a sketchbook priced £3.75. How much change will she receive?

Q2) Angle X is 55 degrees. What is the size of angle W?

Q3) On Monday, the temperature is 4°C. By Tuesday, it has dropped to -1°C. The temperature drops by twice as much from Tuesday to Wednesday. What is the temperature on Wednesday?

Q4) Use the formula below to find the size of angle m if n = 56°.
m = (180 − n) ÷ 2

Q5) Emily has some pocket money. She spends 70% of it and is left with £9.00. How much money did she start with?

Q6) Oliver scored 14/25 in his Maths quiz. What is this as a percentage?

Q7) Lily buys 5 books costing £3.49 each and 4 bookmarks costing 39p each. She estimates: 5×£3.50 + 4×£0.40. How does her estimate differ from the exact cost?

Q8) How many degrees does the minute hand on a clock turn through between 3:00 PM and 11:15 PM?

Q9) Dennis wins £400. He spends £150 on a tablet and buys books costing £25.50 each. Which expression gives the money left if he buys b books?

Q10) Using the previous question, what is the highest number of books Dennis can buy after purchasing his tablet?

Q11) What is 4 − (4 + 4 ÷ 4)?

Q12) Four bells ring at intervals of 2, 8, 7, and 11 seconds. If they all ring at the same time, how many seconds before they all ring together again?

Q13) Philip has £50. He spends £7.25 on a book, £12.50 on a meal, and £4.75 on a movie ticket. What percentage of his money does he have left?

Q14) Two rectangles measuring 10 cm × 14 cm are placed together to create a shape with horizontal and vertical lines of symmetry. What is the perimeter of the shape?
